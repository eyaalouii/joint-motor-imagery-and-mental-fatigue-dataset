# joint-motor-imagery-and-mental-fatigue-dataset
The dataset include 5 healthy participants (5 males, , age range 22--33 years), with normal vision and no reported neurological disorders. All participants provided informed consent prior to the experiment.

To ensure robustness and variability in the data, each participant completed at least four recording sessions conducted on different days. This design allows capturing both inter-subject and inter-session variability, which is essential for developing generalizable models.

Experiments were conducted in a quiet indoor environment with controlled lighting conditions to minimize external disturbances. Participants were comfortably seated in front of a computer screen at a fixed distance, and instructed to remain still and avoid unnecessary movements during data acquisition to reduce motion artifacts in the EEG signals.
EEG signals were recorded using the OpenBCI Cyton board extended with the OpenBCI Daisy Module and mounted on an OpenBCI Ultracortex Headset. This configuration provides up to 16 EEG channels and enables flexible, non-invasive brain signal acquisition suitable for experimental BCI studies. Electrodes were placed following the international 10–20 EEG System. In particular, the electrodes used in this study were dry EEG electrodes having prongs with a length of 5 mm to accommodate longer hair while enabling excellent signal quality. 
The electrodes were mounted in the following locations:
Fp1, Fp2, F3, Fz, F4, C3, Cz, C4, T7, T8, P3, Pz, P4, O1, Oz and O2, along with ear reference electrode
